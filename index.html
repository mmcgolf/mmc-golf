Methodology

Every golfer has an ownership rate — the percentage of teams in the pool that picked them. A golfer selected by 5 of 182 teams has 2.7% ownership; one on 40 teams has 22%. The inverse — how many teams didn't pick them — is their exclusivity.

The Weighted Rating (primary) multiplies each pick's exclusivity by its share of the team's 72-point budget before averaging. A 14-point player picked by 5% of teams contributes roughly twice as much to your rating as an 8-point player at the same ownership, because that expensive pick defines your roster more fundamentally. Click any team to see the per-golfer breakdown.

The Raw Score (secondary) treats all 6 picks equally regardless of PPV — a simple average of exclusivities. Comparing the two tells a story: a big gap means your uniqueness is concentrated either in your stars (weighted > raw) or in your value picks (raw > weighted).

Rivals counts teams sharing 3 or more golfers with you. High rival counts mean your fate is tied to many others — your wins are their wins too.

A high rating isn't inherently better — popular picks are popular for a reason. But if you're hunting for a big payday, a contrarian roster gives you more separation from the field when your guys run hot.
